A brief summary of Stan Lee's life:

  • 1922: Born in Manhattan, Nueva York.
  • 1939: He became an assistant at the new Timely Comics (Marvel Comics) comic-book publisher Martin Goodman's company.
  • 1941: Stanley Lieber made his comic-book debut with the text filler "Captain America Foils the Traitor's Revenge" in Captain America Comics #3 (cover-dated May 1941), using the pseudonym Stan Lee.
  • 1942: He entered the United States Army and served within the US as a member of the Signal Corps, repairing telegraph poles and other communications equipment.
  • 1960s: Again working with Kirby, Lee co-created the Hulk, Thor, Iron Man, and the X-Men; with Bill Everett, Daredevil; and with Steve Ditko, Doctor Strange and Marvel's most successful character, Spider-Man, all of whom lived in a thoroughly shared universe.
  • 1972: Lee stopped writing monthly comic books to assume the role of publisher. His final issue of The Amazing Spider-Man was #110 (July 1972)[105] and his last Fantastic Four was #125 (August 1972).
  • 2018: dies in Los Angeles, California.
